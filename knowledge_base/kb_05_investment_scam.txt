SECTION 1: WHAT IS AN INVESTMENT SCAM?
================================================================================

An investment scam is a type of fraud where criminals offer victims fake or
fraudulent investment opportunities promising extraordinarily high returns with
little or no risk. Victims are lured with the promise of becoming wealthy quickly
through investment in stocks, foreign exchange (forex), cryptocurrency, gold,
unit trusts, or other financial instruments.

In Malaysia, investment scams are the HIGHEST-LOSS category of financial fraud.
The Securities Commission Malaysia (SC) and Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) receive
thousands of complaints annually. Losses can range from thousands to millions
of ringgit per victim.

Malaysian investment scams commonly operate through:
- Telegram and WhatsApp investment groups
- Social media platforms (Facebook, Instagram, TikTok)
- Fake trading platforms (apps or web-based)
- Pyramid and multi-level marketing (MLM) schemes
- Cryptocurrency investment platforms

Key regulatory fact: ALL persons giving investment advice or managing investments
in Malaysia must be LICENSED by the Securities Commission Malaysia (SC) or
Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM). Investing with unlicensed individuals or entities
is illegal and unprotected.

SECTION 2: HOW INVESTMENT SCAMS WORK (STEP BY STEP)
================================================================================

STEP 1 - INITIAL CONTACT AND RECRUITMENT
Scammer contacts victim via:
- Random WhatsApp or Telegram message (often a "wrong number" opener)
- Social media ad featuring fake celebrities endorsing investment scheme
- Friend referral (victim's friend was already scammed and unknowingly recruits others)
- Dating app — scammer poses as romantic interest who then introduces investment
  (this variant is called "Pig Butchering" or Love + Investment scam)

STEP 2 - TRUST BUILDING
Scammer spends time building a relationship and credibility:
- Shows screenshots of "profits" and fake bank statements
- Shares "expert analysis" and market predictions
- Introduces victim to a private "VIP investment group" with active participants
  (all are fake accounts controlled by the syndicate)
- Claims to have insider information or proprietary trading software

STEP 3 - SMALL INITIAL INVESTMENT
Victim is encouraged to invest a small amount (RM500–RM2,000).
The fake platform shows the investment "growing" rapidly.
Victim is allowed to withdraw a small profit to build confidence.

STEP 4 - ESCALATING INVESTMENT
Emboldened by early success, victim invests larger amounts.
Friends and family may be asked to contribute. Some victims take out loans.
The fake platform continues to show unrealistically high returns.

STEP 5 - WITHDRAWAL BLOCK
When victim tries to withdraw a large amount, they are told:
- "You must pay a withdrawal tax of 20% before release"
- "Your account needs to be upgraded — pay RM5,000 for VIP tier"
- "Our system is under maintenance — wait 30 days"
- "You owe a processing fee — pay first then funds will be released"

Every additional payment is extracted from the victim before the scammer disappears.

STEP 6 - TOTAL LOSS
Platform becomes inaccessible. Scammer stops responding. All deposited funds are lost.
Total loss often significantly exceeds initial investment due to escalating payments.

SECTION 3: TYPES OF INVESTMENT SCAMS
================================================================================

TYPE 1 - FOREX / CRYPTOCURRENCY TRADING SCAM (Most Common High-Loss Scam)
Victim is introduced to a "professional trader" or AI trading bot.
Fake trading platform shows extraordinary returns (50–200% monthly).
Victim cannot independently verify positions or trades on the fake platform.
Key identifier: Real forex brokers are regulated by SC Malaysia or BNM.
No legitimate broker guarantees fixed returns.

TYPE 2 - PIG BUTCHERING SCAM (Sha Zhu Pan)
Long-term relationship (romantic or friendly) is built over weeks or months.
Once trust is established, scammer introduces the investment.
Victim is gradually encouraged to invest more and more (being "fattened").
When investment is large enough, the scammer disappears ("slaughter").
Key identifier: Someone you met online who is both romantically interested AND
pushes you to invest in a specific platform they recommend.

TYPE 3 - PONZI SCHEME
Returns to earlier investors are paid using money from newer investors.
Scheme is sustainable only as long as new investors keep joining.
When recruitment slows, scheme collapses and later investors lose everything.
Key identifier: Returns that seem unusually high and consistent regardless of
market conditions. Pressure to recruit others.

TYPE 4 - PYRAMID SCHEME / ILLEGAL MLM
Participants earn primarily by recruiting new members rather than selling products.
Income is dependent on downline recruitment, not product sales.
Key identifier: If the product or service is secondary to recruitment commissions,
it may be an illegal pyramid scheme. Check the MLM company's legitimacy with SC Malaysia.

TYPE 5 - FAKE CRYPTOCURRENCY EXCHANGE / WALLET SCAM
Victim is directed to deposit cryptocurrency into a fake exchange or wallet.
Platform shows balance growing — but it is fake. Funds cannot be withdrawn.
Key identifier: Only use cryptocurrency exchanges registered with SC Malaysia
or internationally recognised regulated exchanges.

TYPE 6 - FIXED DEPOSIT / HIGH-YIELD INVESTMENT SCAM
Victim is offered "fixed deposit" or "high-yield savings" products by unlicensed
entities with returns far higher than bank rates (e.g., 15–30% monthly).
Key identifier: Legitimate Malaysian banks offer FD rates of 2–4% per year, not per month.
Any returns significantly above this are a major warning sign.

TYPE 7 - GOLD / COMMODITY INVESTMENT SCAM
Scammer sells certificates for gold or commodities at inflated prices.
Returns are promised based on commodity price movements.
Physical gold may not exist, or the price claimed is far above market value.
Key identifier: Verify gold price at BNM's official page or major exchanges.
Only buy gold investment products from LBMA-accredited dealers or licensed banks.

TYPE 8 - CELEBRITY-ENDORSED SCAM
Scammer uses fake social media posts, deepfake videos, or edited screenshots
to falsely show that famous Malaysians (politicians, businesspeople, celebrities)
are endorsing a specific investment.
Key identifier: Verify celebrity endorsements directly on their official verified accounts.

SECTION 4: RED FLAGS AND WARNING SIGNS
================================================================================

UNREALISTIC RETURNS:
- Promised returns of 10% per week, 30% per month, or "guaranteed" profits
- Profits that are consistent regardless of market conditions
- "Risk-free" or "zero risk" investment (no legitimate investment is risk-free)
- Returns far higher than any regulated bank or investment product in Malaysia

UNLICENSED OPERATORS:
- Investment operator is not listed on SC Malaysia's licensed entities list
- Operator cannot provide a valid SC or BNM licence number
- Operator only communicates via WhatsApp or Telegram — no physical office
- Company is not registered with SSM (Companies Commission of Malaysia)

PRESSURE AND SECRECY:
- Pressure to invest quickly ("limited slots", "promotion ends today")
- Told to keep the investment secret or not tell family members
- Asked to recruit friends or family for additional returns
- Discouraged from consulting financial advisors or family

WITHDRAWAL PROBLEMS:
- Withdrawal requests are delayed, rejected, or subject to sudden new fees
- Asked to pay "taxes", "processing fees", or "unlock fees" before withdrawal
- Platform goes offline or becomes inaccessible when you try to withdraw
- Customer support becomes unreachable or unresponsive

PLATFORM CONCERNS:
- Platform is a mobile app downloaded from outside Play Store / App Store
- App does not appear on official stores or is very new with few reviews
- Platform website has no physical address, regulation information, or company details
- Balance on the platform looks too good — numbers rise too steadily

SECTION 5: RISK ASSESSMENT RULES (IF-THEN PRODUCTION RULES)
================================================================================

RULE IV-01 [HIGH / CRITICAL RISK]:
IF user is being asked to pay a fee (tax, upgrade, processing) to withdraw their
investment returns THEN risk level is HIGH. If the user already paid the
withdrawal fee/tax/deposit or already transferred investment funds and cannot
withdraw, THEN risk level is CRITICAL.
ACTION: Stop all payments. This may be the final stage of a scam. No legitimate
investment platform should pressure users to pay extra fees through unofficial
channels to release their own money.

RULE IV-02 [HIGH / CRITICAL RISK]:
IF someone user met online (romantically or as a new friend) is recommending a
specific investment platform AND user is developing emotional attachment THEN
risk level is HIGH because this may match the Pig Butchering pattern. If the user
already transferred money, paid fees, or cannot withdraw funds, THEN risk level
is CRITICAL.
ACTION: Immediately cease investment activity. Seek advice from family or trusted
friends. Report to PDRM if money has been transferred.

RULE IV-03 [HIGH RISK]:
IF the investment operator CANNOT provide an SC Malaysia or BNM licence number
OR is not listed on SC's official registered entities list THEN risk level is HIGH.
ACTION: Do NOT invest. Check SC Malaysia's list of licensed entities at sc.com.my
before committing any funds.

RULE IV-04 [HIGH RISK]:
IF investment promises returns of more than 10% per month or "guaranteed" returns
regardless of market conditions THEN risk level is HIGH.
ACTION: Such returns are mathematically unsustainable. This is a classic scam promise.
No legitimate investment guarantees these returns.

RULE IV-05 [HIGH RISK]:
IF investment platform requires download of an app from outside the official app store
OR the app has very few reviews or downloads THEN risk level is HIGH.
ACTION: Do NOT download. Verify legitimacy on SC Malaysia's InvestSmart portal.

RULE IV-06 [MEDIUM RISK]:
IF user's friend or family member is promoting an investment scheme AND pressure
is applied to join quickly THEN risk level is MEDIUM.
ACTION: Even referrals from known persons can be scams — the referrer may also
be a victim. Independently verify the investment with SC Malaysia before joining.

RULE IV-07 [MEDIUM RISK]:
IF user sees a social media ad featuring a celebrity endorsing an investment platform
THEN risk level is MEDIUM.
ACTION: Verify the celebrity endorsement on their official, verified accounts.
Check if the investment operator is licensed by SC Malaysia.

SECTION 6: HOW TO VERIFY AN INVESTMENT IS LEGITIMATE
================================================================================

STEP 1 - CHECK SC MALAYSIA'S LICENSED ENTITIES LIST:
Visit: sc.com.my — go to "Investor Relations" > "Registered and Licensed Persons/Entities"
All legitimate investment advisors, fund managers, and brokers must be listed here.
If the person or company is not on this list, they are NOT licensed to give investment
advice or manage money in Malaysia.

STEP 2 - CHECK BNM'S FINANCIAL CONSUMER ALERT LIST:
BNM maintains a list of unlicensed financial companies and schemes:
Visit: bnm.gov.my — search "Financial Consumer Alert"
This list is updated regularly with known scams and unlicensed operators.

STEP 3 - CHECK SC MALAYSIA'S INVESTOR ALERT LIST:
SC Malaysia also maintains an investor alert list of unauthorised investments:
Visit: sc.com.my/investor-alert
If the company or scheme appears here, it is confirmed fraudulent.

STEP 4 - USE SC MALAYSIA'S INVESTSMART PORTAL:
Visit: investsmart.sc.com.my
This portal provides tools to verify licensed intermediaries, read investor education
materials, and check investment opportunities.

STEP 5 - VERIFY RETURNS ARE REALISTIC:
Compare offered returns against current Malaysian fixed deposit rates (approximately
2–4% per year at major banks). If offered returns are significantly higher, be
extremely cautious and verify thoroughly.

STEP 6 - GOOGLE THE INVESTMENT:
Search for the company name + "scam" or "complaint" or "review Malaysia".
Check if the company appears on consumer forums or news articles as a scam.

SECTION 7: WHAT TO DO IF YOU ARE A VICTIM
================================================================================

STOP INVESTING IMMEDIATELY:
1. Cease all further payments — every additional payment increases your loss
2. Do not believe promises that paying more will recover your funds

TAKE ACTION:
1. Report to SC Malaysia: 03-6204 8999 or via eTribuan at sc.com.my
2. Report to BNM TELELINK: 1-300-88-5465 / +603-2174-1717
3. Report to PDRM CCID: ccid.rmp.gov.my or 03-2610 1559 / 03-2610 1599
4. If money was recently transferred: call NSRC 997 between 8:00am and 8:00pm daily
5. Report to MCMC if scam was via online platform: aduan.mcmc.gov.my

PRESERVE EVIDENCE:
- Screenshots of all conversations, transaction records, platform balance
- All receipts, bank transfer confirmations, and invoices
- Names, phone numbers, and WhatsApp IDs used by scammers
- Screenshots of the fake investment platform
- All evidence needed for police investigation and potential civil claim

FINANCIAL RECOVERY OPTIONS:
- Contact your bank to dispute transactions if made recently
- Consult a lawyer about potential civil recovery actions
- Speak to a financial counsellor at AKPK (Credit Counselling and Debt Management
  Agency): akpk.org.my | 1-800-88-2575 (free service for debt advice)
- Bank Negara can assist with unresolved complaints through the Ombudsman for
  Financial Services: ofs.org.my

PSYCHOLOGICAL SUPPORT:
- Investment scam victims often experience shame, depression, and financial stress
- Seek support from family, friends, or mental health professionals
- Contact Befrienders Malaysia for emotional support: 03-7627-2929

SECTION 8: KNOW YOUR RIGHTS AS AN INVESTOR
================================================================================

REGULATORY FRAMEWORK IN MALAYSIA:
- Securities Commission Malaysia (SC): Regulates capital markets, investment advisors,
  fund managers, stock brokers, and equity crowdfunding platforms.
  Authority under: Capital Markets and Services Act (CMSA) 2007

- Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM): Regulates banking institutions, insurance companies,
  money services businesses, and financial products offered by banks.
  Authority under: Financial Services Act (FSA) 2013

- Companies Commission of Malaysia (SSM): Registers all companies.
  Verify company legitimacy at ssm.com.my.

INVESTOR RIGHTS:
- You have the right to demand proof of licence from any person giving investment advice
- You have the right to independently verify any investment before committing funds
- You have the right to report any unlicensed investment activity to SC or BNM
- You have the right to a formal investigation and response when you file a complaint

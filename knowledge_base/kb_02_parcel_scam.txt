SECTION 1: WHAT IS A FAKE PARCEL DELIVERY SCAM?
================================================================================

A fake parcel delivery scam (also called parcel or courier scam) is a type of fraud
where scammers impersonate legitimate courier or postal services to deceive victims
into paying fake fees, revealing banking credentials, or downloading malware.

In Malaysia, this is one of the fastest-growing scam categories. Scammers commonly
impersonate:
- Pos Malaysia
- J&T Express
- DHL
- FedEx
- Ninja Van
- GDex
- Lazada Logistics / Shopee Express

These scams exploited the surge in e-commerce and online shopping during and after
the COVID-19 pandemic as millions of Malaysians shop online regularly.

The typical Malaysian victim profile: online shoppers who frequently receive parcels,
often from platforms like Shopee and Lazada.

SECTION 2: HOW FAKE PARCEL DELIVERY SCAMS WORK (STEP BY STEP)
================================================================================

STEP 1 - FAKE NOTIFICATION
Victim receives an SMS, WhatsApp message, or email claiming a parcel is on the way
but cannot be delivered. The message appears to come from a real courier service.
Examples:
- "Your parcel cannot be delivered. Update your address to reschedule."
- "Your package is being held at customs. Pay RM12 customs duty to release."
- "Delivery failed. Click here to reschedule: [fake link]"

STEP 2 - PHISHING LINK
The message contains a link to a fake website that mimics the courier's official site.
The fake site may ask the victim to:
a) Enter personal details to "reschedule" delivery
b) Pay a small fee (RM5–RM30) via banking transfer or e-wallet
c) Enter credit card details for delivery fee payment
d) Download a "tracking app" (actually malware or RAT)

STEP 3 - DATA AND MONEY THEFT
If victim pays the fee — the money goes directly to the scammer.
If victim enters banking credentials — the scammer uses them for full account access.
If victim downloads the fake app — scammer gains remote control of device.

STEP 4 - ESCALATION (ADVANCED VARIANTS)
In more sophisticated versions:
- Scammer calls victim claiming to be from customs or courier company
- They inform victim a "suspicious package" was found in their name
- They transfer victim to a fake "police officer" who demands investigation cooperation
- Victim is pressured to transfer large sums to a "safe account" (mule account)
- This variant can cause losses of RM10,000 to RM500,000+

SECTION 3: TYPES OF FAKE PARCEL DELIVERY SCAMS
================================================================================

TYPE 1 - FAILED DELIVERY SMS/WHATSAPP SCAM (Most Common in Malaysia)
Victim receives fake SMS claiming parcel delivery failed and must click link to
reschedule. Link leads to phishing site asking for personal info or small payment.
Key identifier: Unexpected message for a parcel you did not order.

TYPE 2 - CUSTOMS DUTY SCAM
Victim is told a parcel was intercepted at customs and must pay duty fees.
Payment is requested via bank transfer, e-wallet, or cryptocurrency.
Key identifier: Real customs duty notices come via official channels, not WhatsApp.

TYPE 3 - FAKE TRACKING APP SCAM
Victim is asked to download a courier tracking app via APK link (not from Play Store).
The APK is malware that steals banking credentials, SMS OTPs, and contact lists.
Key identifier: Real courier companies never ask you to install an APK via SMS.

TYPE 4 - FAKE CUSTOMS + POLICE SCAM (Syndikat Macau Scam variant)
Victim is told their parcel was flagged for illegal items. A fake police officer
calls to "investigate" and instructs victim to transfer money to a "safe account."
Key identifier: Police NEVER contact civilians via WhatsApp or ask for bank transfers.

TYPE 5 - WRONG PARCEL / REFUND SCAM
Scammer claims a parcel was sent to victim by mistake and requests banking details
to process a "refund." Instead of sending money, they steal banking credentials.
Key identifier: Why would a courier need your full banking credentials for a refund?

TYPE 6 - PRIZE/REWARD PARCEL SCAM
Victim is told they won a prize and a parcel is being sent, but they must pay
"delivery charges" or "insurance fees" to receive it.
Key identifier: Legitimate prize winners do not pay fees to receive their prizes.

SECTION 4: RED FLAGS AND WARNING SIGNS
================================================================================

UNEXPECTED PARCEL NOTIFICATION:
- You receive a notification for a parcel you never ordered or are not expecting
- The notification arrives via WhatsApp or Telegram (official couriers use SMS/email)
- There is no tracking number provided or the tracking number cannot be verified
- The message addresses you generically ("Dear Customer" instead of your name)

SUSPICIOUS LINK:
- Link does not match official courier website (e.g., pos-malaysia.com.my vs pos.com.my)
- URL uses free hosting platforms or random character domains
- Link is shortened (bit.ly, tinyurl) hiding the real destination
- Website asks for personal information not needed for delivery rescheduling
- Website loads slowly or has poor visual quality/missing graphics

REQUESTS FOR PAYMENT:
- Asked to pay small "customs fees", "duty charges", or "redelivery fees" via bank transfer
- Payment must be made urgently "within 24 hours" or parcel will be returned
- Payment requested via e-wallet (Touch 'n Go, GrabPay) instead of official payment gateway
- Courier asks you to pay via cryptocurrency
- Any upfront payment request for a parcel you did not order

REQUESTS FOR SENSITIVE INFORMATION:
- Fake courier asks for your full name, IC number, and address (beyond rescheduling needs)
- Asked to provide banking details or OTP to "process refund" or "verify identity"
- Asked to download an app to "track your parcel" from an unofficial source

PHONE CALLS:
- Caller claims to be from customs or police regarding your parcel
- Caller says illegal items (drugs, fake documents) were found in your parcel
- Caller instructs you to keep the conversation confidential or face legal trouble
- You are transferred between multiple "officers" — a common high-pressure tactic
- Caller threatens arrest, court action, or frozen bank accounts

SECTION 5: RISK ASSESSMENT RULES (IF-THEN PRODUCTION RULES)
================================================================================

RULE PA-01 [CRITICAL RISK]:
IF user received a call from someone claiming to be police or customs AND they say
illegal items were found in a parcel linked to user's name AND they request bank
transfer to a "safe account" THEN risk level is CRITICAL.
ACTION: Hang up immediately. This is the Macau Scam variant. Report to PDRM 999
and NSRC 997 immediately.

RULE PA-02 [HIGH RISK]:
IF user received an SMS or WhatsApp about parcel delivery AND the message contains
a link AND the link leads to a site asking for banking credentials or OTP THEN
risk level is HIGH.
ACTION: Do NOT enter any details. Do not click. Verify directly with courier using
their official website or customer service hotline.

RULE PA-03 [HIGH RISK]:
IF user is asked to download an APK file or tracking app from a link in a message
(not from official Google Play Store or Apple App Store) THEN risk level is HIGH.
ACTION: Do NOT download. APK files from unknown sources are commonly malware or RATs.

RULE PA-04 [HIGH RISK]:
IF user received a request to pay customs duty, delivery fee, or any charge for
a parcel they did not order THEN risk level is HIGH.
ACTION: Do NOT pay. Verify directly with the official courier company using
their official hotline.

RULE PA-05 [MEDIUM RISK]:
IF user received a parcel delivery notification AND the tracking number cannot be
verified on the courier's official website THEN risk level is MEDIUM.
ACTION: Do not click any links. Go to the official courier website directly and
enter the tracking number manually.

RULE PA-06 [MEDIUM RISK]:
IF user received a "missed delivery" notification AND the sender information does
not match any expected parcels THEN risk level is MEDIUM.
ACTION: Contact the courier using their official customer service number before
taking any action.

RULE PA-07 [CRITICAL - POST-INCIDENT]:
IF user has already paid a delivery or customs fee to a suspected scammer THEN
risk level is CRITICAL — financial loss has occurred.
ACTION: Call NSRC 997 immediately. Report to PDRM ccid.rmp.gov.my. Contact bank
to dispute transaction. Preserve all communication as evidence.

RULE PA-08 [CRITICAL - POST-INCIDENT]:
IF user has installed an APK app from a suspicious link THEN risk level is CRITICAL.
ACTION: Immediately factory reset the device OR use antivirus to scan and remove
malware. Call bank to freeze account. Change all passwords from a clean device.

SECTION 6: HOW TO VERIFY A PARCEL NOTIFICATION IS LEGITIMATE
================================================================================

STEP 1 - CHECK TRACKING NUMBER:
A legitimate delivery notification always includes a real tracking number.
Go directly to the courier's official website (type the URL yourself, do not click
the link in the message) and enter the tracking number.

Official tracking websites:
- Pos Malaysia: pos.com.my
- J&T Express: jtexpress.my
- DHL: dhl.com/my
- FedEx: fedex.com/en-my
- Ninja Van: ninjavan.co/en-my
- GDex: gdexpress.com

STEP 2 - CROSS-CHECK WITH YOUR ORDER HISTORY:
Log into Shopee, Lazada, or whichever platform you use. Check if you have any
pending orders with a matching estimated delivery date. If no matching order exists,
the parcel notification is likely fake.

STEP 3 - CALL THE COURIER DIRECTLY:
Use the courier's official hotline (found on their official website) to verify
whether a parcel is truly in transit under your name.
Do NOT use the phone number provided in the suspicious message.

STEP 4 - CHECK SENDER:
Official Pos Malaysia SMS comes from "Pos Malaysia" sender ID.
If an SMS claims to be from Pos Malaysia but the sender is a phone number like
+60123456789 or a random name, it is fake.

SECTION 7: WHAT TO DO IF YOU ARE A VICTIM
================================================================================

IF YOU PAID A FEE (financial loss):
1. Call NSRC 997 immediately — 24/7 hotline to freeze mule accounts
2. Call your bank's fraud hotline to dispute the transaction and freeze account
3. File a police report at PDRM (ccid.rmp.gov.my or nearest police station)
4. Report to MCMC at aduan.mcmc.gov.my

IF YOU ENTERED YOUR BANKING CREDENTIALS ON A FAKE SITE:
1. Call your bank immediately to freeze account and change credentials
2. Change all passwords from a clean, uncompromised device
3. Monitor bank statements for unauthorised transactions
4. Report to NSRC 997 and PDRM

IF YOU INSTALLED A SUSPICIOUS APP:
1. Do NOT open any banking apps on the compromised device
2. Factory reset your device (WARNING: back up data to a different device first)
3. Call your bank to freeze accounts from a clean device
4. Enable enhanced security on banking apps (if available)
5. Report to MyCERT at mycert.org.my

IF YOU SPOKE TO A FAKE POLICE OFFICER / CUSTOMS OFFICER:
1. Stop all communication immediately
2. Note down all details: phone numbers, names used, what was said
3. Report to real PDRM: 999 (emergency) or 03-2610-1559 (CCID)
4. If money was transferred, call NSRC 997 immediately
